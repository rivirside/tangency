Mutual Aid Drop Spot Playbook

Launch a pop-up hub where neighbors can leave or pick up essentials with dignity and zero bureaucracy.

Focus: Mutual Aid Format: Playbook Time: 2–3 hours setup Team: 4–8 hosts

Perfect for Weekends, holiday drives, neighborhood events, or workplace giving days.

Deliverable Organized intake, sorting, and community shopping zones.

Materials Folding tables, signage, bins, label tape, QR feedback form.

Outputs Inventory log + list of unmet requests.

Prep checklist

  • Secure a location with shade or indoor access, restrooms, and nearby transit.
  • Obtain permission from property owners or city permitting offices if needed.
  • Create clear signage for “Intake,” “Sorting,” and “Free Store” zones.
  • Set up a simple QR survey or clipboard to capture requests and feedback.

Operating the drop spot

  1. Intake. Greeters welcome donors, record items, and note expiration dates if food is involved.
  2. Sorting. Volunteers inspect items quickly, wipe them down, and sort by category (pantry, hygiene, baby, PPE, etc.).
  3. Community shopping. Display items neatly with category cards. Offer tote bags and let neighbors choose what they need without limits unless inventory is low.
  4. Wrap-up. Document what’s left, redistribute perishable goods, and share requests on social so donors know what to bring next time.

Care tips

  • Post your mutual aid values (solidarity not charity, no questions asked) so everyone understands the culture.
  • Budget for stipends or gift cards for directly impacted volunteers who are holding the space.
  • Partner with local businesses for recurring donations of high-need items.
