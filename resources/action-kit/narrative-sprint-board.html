Narrative Sprint Board

Guide your communications team through a focused week-long content sprint that keeps every channel on message and ends with measurable outputs.

Focus: Communications Format: Sprint Template Time: 60 min kickoff + async updates Team: Comms lead + partners

Perfect for Launch weeks, report drops, or moments when rapid response overlaps with planned content.

Deliverable Shared Kanban or spreadsheet with status for each post, pitch, and email.

Materials Project board (Trello, Airtable, Notion), brand assets, approval matrix.

Outputs Consistent story arc + analytics recap.

Before you start

Define the single argument you need people to remember this week. Draft two proof points and one call-to-action. Everything placed on the board must ladder back to those pillars.

Prep checklist

  • Create the sprint board with columns for Idea, Drafting, Review, Scheduled, and Live.
  • List every channel you control: email, SMS, IG, TikTok, press list, podcast, etc.
  • Gather existing assets (photos, impact stats, quotes) into one shared folder.
  • Confirm who can approve copy, graphics, and spokespeople so there’s no bottleneck mid-sprint.

Running the sprint

  1. Kickoff (Day 0, 60 min). Walk through the message brief, assign owners to each channel, and time-box production windows.
  2. Async stand-ups (Daily, 10 min). Use Slack threads or Loom updates for blockers and quick wins. Only schedule a meeting if multiple pieces stall.
  3. Amplify proof (Mid-week). Share early analytics so the team can double down on what’s resonating. Encourage remixing high performers for slower channels.
  4. Close + retro (End of week, 30 min). Capture what worked, what flopped, and the templates you want to reuse. Export the board as documentation for partners.

Tips

  • Pin your key talking points at the top of every channel-specific task so editors keep them in view.
  • Store captions, graphics, and alt text inside the card so future campaigns can repurpose quickly.
  • Use UTM links or simple tracking codes to tie conversions back to the sprint.
