Power Mapping Lab

Run a 90-minute working session that surfaces every decision-maker, influencer, and ally connected to your campaign so you can aim pressure precisely.

Focus: Campaign Strategy Format: Workshop Agenda Time: 90 minutes Team: 3–20 organizers

Perfect for Campaign kick-offs, board strategy retreats, coalition alignment meetings.

Deliverable Annotated power map + ownership grid for follow-up research.

Materials Large paper or Miro board, sticky notes, markers, projector.

Outputs Target tiers, influencer list, assigned researchers.

How to use this kit

  1. Print the workbook. Use the HTML workbook link below and “Print to PDF” or a physical copy.
  2. Run a roster brainstorm. Fill the first page before you ever open a mapping tool.
  3. Complete the target profile. Assign a team owner and document pressure points.
  4. Plot targets on the strategy grid. Once you fill the matrix, convert it into a digital map or Miro board.
  5. Refresh after every escalation. Update the workbook each time you gain intel or shift tactics.

Key terms

  • Primary target: The single person or institution with the power to grant your demand.
  • Secondary targets: People who influence the primary target—donors, advisors, regulators, board members.
  • Impacted vs. influence: “Impacted” captures how directly the issue touches someone; “Influence” describes their ability to change the outcome.
  • Leverage lines: Relationships or pressure points (funders, public opinion, regulations) you can activate to shift the target.

Why this lab matters

Without a power map you end up guessing where to aim your tactics. This session slows everyone down long enough to agree on the specific person with authority to give your demand, the people whispering in their ear, and which allies are underutilized. The final artifact becomes your campaign’s north star.

Prep checklist

  • Invite a diverse mix of staff, volunteers, and directly impacted members.
  • Collect any research you already have on decision-makers or corporate structure.
  • Print or duplicate the Tangency Power Map canvas so participants can add notes quickly.
  • Assign a facilitator, timekeeper, note-taker, and photographer before the meeting begins.

Step-by-step agenda

  1. Frame the win (10 min). Share the crisp demand, the deadline, and what “victory” unlocks. Ask participants to name the one person who can say yes.
  2. Map the primary + secondary targets (25 min). Place the primary decision-maker in the center. Brainstorm every person or institution that influences them—funders, customers, professional associations—placing them closer or further away based on strength of relationship.
  3. Layer allies, opponents, and neutrals (20 min). Add organizations, community leaders, and internal champions. Mark allies with green, opponents with red, undecided targets with yellow.
  4. Assign ownership (20 min). For each influential node, decide who will research, cultivate, or pressure them. Capture every assignment on a shared tracker before the session ends.
  5. Close with next steps (15 min). Summarize insights, schedule the first follow-up touch points, and photograph the map from multiple angles. Share the files within 24 hours.

Facilitator tips

  • Keep people moving. Stand-up energy keeps brainstorming lively and surfaces bolder ideas.
  • Push for specificity—“City Council” is vague, “Council President Ortega” is actionable.
  • After the session convert the map into a living diagram (Miro, Google Slides) so distant teammates can contribute.
