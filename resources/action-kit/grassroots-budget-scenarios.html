Grassroots Budget Scenario Builder

Stress-test your next fiscal year by modeling conservative, likely, and stretch scenarios with transparent tradeoffs.

Budgets are strategy documents. When you know exactly what a stretch gift funds or what a revenue dip forces you to cut, staff and board can react quickly. This kit helps you build those scenarios in one sitting so decisions feel less mysterious.

Focus: Fundraising & Operations Format: Spreadsheet Template Time: 60 minutes Team: Finance + program owners

Perfect for Budget approvals, board meetings, grant reporting, or campaign pivots.

Deliverable Linked spreadsheet with three income/expense scenarios and narrative summary.

Materials Accounting exports, grant calendar, staffing plan.

Outputs Decision memo: what we greenlight or pause under each scenario.

Prep checklist

  • List non-negotiable fixed costs (insurance, software, compliance fees).
  • Identify variable costs that scale with programming (travel, stipends, venue rentals).
  • Forecast income streams: grassroots gifts, grants, events, earned revenue.
  • Clarify program priorities so you can quickly map dollars to impact.

Scenario build

  1. Populate the base sheet. Enter last year’s actuals to set baselines.
  2. Create three tabs. Conservative (worst case), likely, and stretch projections with notes on assumptions.
  3. Translate into decisions. For each scenario, write three statements: “We can fund…”, “We delay…”, “We pause…”.
  4. Share + invite input. Review with staff leads and board finance committee so everyone understands the stakes.

Tips

  • Color-code revenue sources so it’s easy to see if you’re over-relying on one stream.
  • Include a cash-on-hand row to monitor runway each quarter.
  • Publish the stretch scenario headline to your community—it can drive targeted sponsorships.

How to use this kit

  1. Pull last year’s actuals. You need real numbers before you model any scenario.
  2. Duplicate the spreadsheet for each scenario. Conservative, likely, and stretch should live on separate tabs.
  3. Translate numbers into decisions. Add statements such as “If conservative, we pause X.”
  4. Share with staff and board. Bring the decision memo to finance committee and frontline leads so everyone sees the stakes.

Key terms

  • Conservative / likely / stretch: Three revenue scenarios ranging from worst-case to ambitious.
  • Runway: How many months of expenses you can cover with current cash on hand.
  • Decision memo: A summary you send to board or funders outlining what each scenario means operationally.
  • Expense levers: Programs or costs you can ramp up or down depending on revenue.
