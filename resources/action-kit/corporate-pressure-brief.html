Corporate Pressure Brief

Turn raw research into a two-page brief that equips supporters to pressure a company, donor, or institutional target.

Corporations move when they feel pressure from multiple pillars at once. A well written pressure brief translates dense research into a shareable roadmap that anyone can use. This kit teaches you how to distill harm, name the demand, and give supporters scripts that target every source of leverage.

Focus: Research + Accountability Format: Brief Template Time: Half-day Team: Researchers + spokespeople

Perfect for Investor meetings, shareholder actions, consumer pressure campaigns.

Deliverable PDF/slide brief with demand, leverage points, scripts.

Materials Company filings, charity ratings, stakeholder quotes.

Outputs Shareable doc for supporters + insider champions.

Prep checklist

  • Collect publicly available data: annual reports, board lists, philanthropic giving.
  • Interview allies inside the company if safe to do so.
  • Map leverage: shareholders, employees, vendors, regulators, key markets.
  • Decide on the specific policy or dollar shift you are demanding.

Brief structure

  1. Page 1: Stakes + demand. Describe the harm, name the affected community, and state the demand in bold type.
  2. Page 1: Leverage matrix. Use a simple table to show each pillar (investors, customers, labor) and the action you’ll take.
  3. Page 2: Scripts + actions. Provide supporter scripts (phone/email) and insider scripts for employees or donors.
  4. Page 2: Receipts. Cite data, quotes, and visuals proving why the company must move now.

Tips

  • Include a heat map that highlights where the company operates—local chapters can tailor actions.
  • Design with shareability in mind; a one-pager can live on phones at rallies or shareholder meetings.
  • Add a QR code that links to a petition or toolkit for rapid mobilization.

How to use this kit

  1. Collect receipts before writing. Pull filings, quotes, and impacted stories so your brief is grounded.
  2. Draft page 1 with the demand + leverage matrix. Make the stakes and pathways obvious at a glance.
  3. Fill page 2 with scripts and proof. Give supporters and insiders instant talking points.
  4. Distribute everywhere. Email to investors, hand out at rallies, and embed on your campaign site.

Key terms

  • Leverage matrix: A table showing which pillars (investors, customers, workers) you can mobilize.
  • Pillars: Groups that underpin a company’s power—shareholders, regulators, employees, consumers.
  • Supporter vs. insider scripts: Public messaging vs. talking points for people who have influence inside the company.
  • Receipts: Verified data, quotes, or visuals that prove the company’s harm or hypocrisy.
